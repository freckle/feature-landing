Math Topics

Front Row provides resources for every classroom
Click on a topic below to learn more.

Counting and Operations ▸

From learning to count, to multiplication in word problems, to solving simple expressions, students get comfortable with their numbers and how they relate to each other.
Grades K-5

Base 10 ▸

From learning numbers above 10, to fluent adding and subtracting, to division with multi-digit divisors, students understand how numbers operate in base ten.
Grades K-5

Measurement and Data ▸

From learning the concept of measurement, to telling time in different units, to calculating the volume of rectillinear solids, students learn different representations of data.
Grades K-5

Geometry ▸

From learning the names of shapes, to partitioning fractions, to learning attributes and categories, students become comfortable interacting with various shapes.
Grades K-5

Fractions ▸

From unit fractions on a number line, to multiplying fractions, to fractional rectangles, students learn how parts relate to a whole in a variety of ways.
Grades 3-5

Ratios and Proportions ▸

From equivalent ratios, to multistep ratio and percent problems, to graphing proportional relationships, students understand how different units relate to each other.
Grades 6 & 7

Number System ▸

From Greatest Common Factors and Least Common Multiples, to division of signed numbers, to approximations of irrational numbers, students get comfortable with how more complex numbers work. Grades 6-8

Advanced Geometry ▸

From volume of rectangular prisms, to circle properties, to angle transformations, students build on their foundations to expand their knowledge of various shapes and how to use them.
Grades 6-8

Expressions and Equations ▸

From evaluating expressions, to different forms of expressions, to slope and similar triangles, students learn how to evaluate abstract representations of numbers.
Grades 6-8

Statistics and Probability ▸

From statistical questions, to probability of compound events, to bivariate categorical data, students learn how to calculate uncertainty and evaluate real-world data.
Grades 6-8

Functions ▸

From comparing functions, to linear relationships, to interpreting functions, students build on math foundations to understand relationships between variables.
Grade 8
