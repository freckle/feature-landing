Why differentiated learning is needed...

In a typical classroom, some students are too advanced for the material being taught while others are struggling to keep up. The traditional method of teaching every student the same lesson plan means some students are left confused while others are left bored. Front Row's differentiated program reaches every student where they are, insuring that they are both engaged and challenged.

What using Front Row looks like...

Both Front Row's Math and ELA programs, available for grades K - 8, include differentiated practice for students.

Students take a diagnostic to determine their current level

Diagnostics are required when starting ELA for the first time and when each new Math domain is started. This allows Front Row to calibrate to each student's starting level.

Students answer questions at their level

As they practice, students earn coins to use to dress up an avatar. Since every student is being challenged, those who are behind have the same chances as students who are ahead to earn coins and appear on the class leaderboard.

Teachers get detailed data reports

As students practice, teachers can access actionable data views that show exactly how each student is doing. This real-time feedback allows teachers to address student pain points as they happen, rather than having to wait until after tests are graded to identify where students are struggling.

Benefits of differentiated learning...

As students work through Front Row's differentiated program, they:

  • Take ownership of their education…
    …when answering differentiated questions and seeing their own growth
  • Learn to collaborate and help their peers…
    …as Front Row suggests classmates who can help when they get stuck
  • Gain confidence…
    …through seeing problems that match their level and are not too difficult for them
  • Get immediate feedback…
    …when they find out right away if they've answered a question correctly
  • Are motivated to practice…
    …by gaining coins to dress up their avatar as they answer questions
  • Have access to effective learning tools…
    …through read-aloud, online manipulatives, and Spanish options
  • Prepare for standardized tests...
    ...by answering questions in the format seen in the major tests
