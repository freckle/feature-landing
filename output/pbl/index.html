What is Project Based Learning?

Project Based Learning (PBL) is a teaching method that engages students in complex, real-world problems and asks them to develop and execute a strategy to solve them. This form of active learning puts students at the forefront of their own education.

Develop 21st Century Skills


Front Row's Inquiry Based Lessons engage students in developing a conceptual understanding of math. These real-world lessons incorporate various Science and Social Studies standards and allow students to critically engage in discussions and presentations as they solve each day's lesson.

Watch a sample 5th grade video below:

Lessons are 3-5 days and are designed to fill a day's math block. Each day, students watch a video that introduces them to the inquiry, work together with classmates to identify and solve the problem, and engage with the class on discussing the best answers.

Foster rich classroom discussion


Front Row's Article Series dive deep into broad topics or themes that cannot be fully explored in a single article. They allow students to dig into related content and establish meaningful connections between people, events, and diverse experiences.

The goals of the Article Series are to foster critical thinking, encourage the synthesis of ideas, and expose students to diverse ideas and opinions while also giving them the opportunity to cultivate their own. Each Article Series culminates in a unit exam, a final writing prompt, and an engaging final project.
