Why Project-Based Learning works...

Project-Based Learning is a learning method that engages students in a complex, real-world problem and asks them to develop and execute a strategy to solve it. This engages them in active learning, putting students at the forefront of their own education.

What a lesson looks like...

Lessons are cross-curricular, incorporating various Science and Social Studies standards, and are designed to fill a day’s math block (55-75 minutes). Each lesson is 3 to 5 days long and comes with a daily presentation, video, and inquiry sheet. Only about 10-15 minutes of prep time is required!

Students are introduced to the lesson

Each daily video explains the context of that day’s lesson. It is important to note that students are not given a question to answer. Rather, students discover for themselves what they want to solve and how to do so.

Students work in groups to discover the inquiry

After the video, students divide into groups of 3-4 and work together to solve the inquiry. Front Row’s lessons follow the concept of “low floor, high ceiling”, which means students at all levels are engaged and challenged.

Students present their results

After working in groups, a representative from each is given the chance to present their findings. Each student is encouraged to engage, thoughtfully explaining why they agree or disagree with the presenter.
