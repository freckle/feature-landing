How Front Row was developed...

In many classrooms, teachers lack sufficient resources to provide the best education they could for their students. Front Row seeks to address this need for teachers through its powerful tools for both Math and ELA. Many of the tools are, and will always be, completely free.

What Front Row looks like...

Front Row consists of personalized practice for both Math and ELA covering grades K - 8 as well as innovative Inquiry Based Lessons for Math covering grades 1 - 8.

Personalized Math Program

Front Row's free Teacher Edition includes the full content library of over 30,000 standards-aligned questions and over 2,000 videos. The ability to assign a specific standard to students as well as most of the teacher data views is also available in the Teacher Edition.

Personalized ELA program

Front Row ELA contains articles spanning topics from Hurricanes to Donuts, with each article available at 5 different reading levels. This ensures that every student can read the same topic recardless of their reading ability. Every teacher on Front Row has access to all of the ELA articles and can assign up to 5 articles for their class a month.

Inquiry Based Lessons

These real-world lessons take students on a journey of math discovery. Rather than telling students what to solve, these lessons allow students to come up with the questions to ask. Through doing this, they discover for themselves why math concepts are important and how they are applicable.

Benefits of Front Row's free program...

Front Row was created to make it as easy as possible for teachers to provide effective, individual education for each student. As students practice, they:

  • Take ownership of their education...
    ...as they answer questions at their own level and can see their growth
  • Prepare for standardized tests
    ...through answering questions in the same format seen in major tests
  • Are motivated to practice...
    ...by gaining coins as they practice to dress up their avatar
  • Gain confidence...
    ...as they see content matched to their level and is not too difficult for them
  • Engage in cross-curricular learning...
    ...through Science and Social Studies-aligned articles and Inquiry Based Lessons
  • Have access to effective learning tools...
    ...in the read-aloud, online manipulatives, and Spanish options
  • Learn to collaborate and help their peers...
    ...when Front Row suggests classmates who can help when they get stuck
