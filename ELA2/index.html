How Front Row ELA was developed...

Literacy is one of the most fundamental pieces of education, and every student deserves tailored education to help them grow in reading skills. Front Row's ELA program allows students at all different levels to read together, making it easy for teachers to provide effective, differentiated ELA education.

What Front Row ELA looks like...

ELA articles cover a broad spectrum of non-fiction topics ranging from Hurricanes to Donuts, providing engaging content for all students. Many articles are cross-curricular with various Social Studies and Science standards as well.

Students take a starting diagnostic

The diagnostic identifies student strengths and weaknesses to ensure that everyone recevies articles tailored to their reading level. As students continue to practice, Front Row automatically adjusts reading levels.

Teachers assign an article topic for students

Each student is automatically set at their recommended reading level as determined by the program, but teachers have the ability to adjust the level as needed.

Teachers get detailed data reports

As students practice, teachers can access actionable data views that show exactly how each student is doing. This real-time feedback allows teachers to address student pain points as they happen, rather than having to wait until after tests are graded to identify where students are struggling.

Benefits of Front Row ELA...

Front Row's ELA program was created to make it as easy as possible for teachers to provide effective, individual education for each student. As students practice, they:

  • Engage in meaningful discussion…
    …as each student is able to read about the same topic regardless of reading level
  • Gain immediate feedback…
    …when they find out right away how they've done on an assignment
  • Are motivated to practice…
    …by gaining coins as they practice to dress up their avatar
  • Gain confidence…
    …as they see articles matched to their level and are not too difficult for them
  • Engage in cross-curricular learning…
    …through reading articles aligned with Science and Social Studies standards
  • Prepare for standardized testing…
    …through Front Row's standards-aligned questions for each article
